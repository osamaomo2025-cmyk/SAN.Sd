## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & GUIDING PRINCIPLES

This document establishes the official **Enterprise Document & Records Management Framework (EDRMF)** for the Sudan Digital Ministry of Commerce & Industry (MCI). Formulated under the national "Sudan 2035 Digital Sovereignty Directive," this architecture is designed to govern the entire lifecycle of physical and digital records generated by citizens, merchants, investors, caseworkers, and automated integrations over the next 20 years.

The EDRMF transitions the Ministry to a **Digital-First, Cryptographically Verified, and legally Binding Information System** that guarantees record integrity, accessibility, compliance, and readiness for AI semantic search.

```
+---------------+     +---------------+     +---------------+     +---------------+
|  1. Capture   | ──► |  2. Classify  | ──► |  3. Secure &  | ──► |  4. Retain &  |
|  (OCR / PDF)  |     |  (Taxonomy)   |     |   Preserve    |     |    Archive    |
+---------------+     +---------------+     +---------------+     +---------------+
```

### Guiding Principles:
*   **Digital by Default:** Every record is captured, classified, and managed digitally. Physical documents, when unavoidable, are immediately digitized, validated using optical character recognition (OCR), and integrated into the digital records repository.
*   **Legal Reliability & Chain of Custody:** Documents generated by or submitted to the platform are treated as official legal evidence. The system maintains an unbroken, cryptographically signed audit trail from initial draft to permanent archive.
*   **Structured Metadata (AI-Ready):** All documents are tagged with rich, bilingual (Arabic & English) metadata, ensuring that the Sovereign AI Assistant (Gemini) can quickly search, summarize, and retrieve relevant administrative files.
*   **Micro-Segmented Access Control:** Document access is governed by precise, role-based, and attribute-based security policies, keeping sensitive corporate and personal records secure.
*   **Configurable Lifecycle Governance:** Retention periods, classification taxonomies, and disposal workflows are managed dynamically through configuration settings, allowing the system to easily adapt to changing legislative requirements.

## 2. RECORDS CLASSIFICATION TAXONOMY

MCI records are organized under a strict hierarchical classification scheme. Every document belongs to exactly one record series, which determines its security level, access controls, and retention schedule.

```
[Ministry Root: MCI] ──► [Sector: SE-01 Trade] ──► [Department: DE-01 Registry] ──► [Series: CR-LLC] ──► [Case Record File]
```

### 2.1 Taxonomy Hierarchy Codes
*   **Level 1: Ministry Root Code (MCI)**
*   **Level 2: Sector Codes (SEC)**
    *   `SEC-CR` - Commercial Registries
    *   `SEC-IL` - Industrial Licensing
    *   `SEC-IS` - Investment Services
    *   `SEC-IE` - Import & Export
    *   `SEC-CP` - Consumer Protection
    *   `SEC-IP` - Inspection Services
    *   `SEC-AD` - Internal Administration & Executive Decisions
*   **Level 3: Department Codes (DEPT)**
    *   `DEPT-REG` - Corporate Registries Office
    *   `DEPT-ENV` - Environmental Compliance Division
    *   `DEPT-ENF` - Enforcement & Citation Agency
    *   `DEPT-EXC` - Executive Secretariat & Minister's Office
*   **Level 4: Record Series Codes (SER)**
    *   `SER-CERT` - Official Certificates & Signed Licenses
    *   `SER-LEGAL` - Legal Articles of Incorporation & Contracts
    *   `SER-EVID` - Photo Evidence and Field Citation Files
    *   `SER-CORR` - Ministerial Decrees, Circulars, and Correspondence
    *   `SER-FIN` - Fee Receipts, Audit Ledgers, and Invoices

## 3. DOCUMENT METADATA SCHEMAS & DICTIONARY

To support search, security, and AI processing, every document must include a standard bilingual metadata envelope.

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│               Sovereign Metadata Envelope              │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Unique ID: doc-81249-108a                              │
│ Title (AR): شهادة تسجيل شركة النيل                       │
│ Title (EN): Blue Nile Registration Certificate          │
│ Record Class: SEC-CR / DEPT-REG / SER-CERT             │
│ Retention Schedule: RET-CR-01 (15 Years Active)        │
│ Security Classification: RESTRICTED                    │
│ Cryptographic Stamp: SHA-256 [a8f3c2...]               │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Standard Metadata Dictionary:

*   **`documentId` (string):** Unique, system-generated alphanumeric ID (e.g., `doc-81249-108a`).
*   **`titleAr` / `titleEn` (string):** Descriptive document titles in Arabic and English.
*   **`classificationCode` (string):** Taxonomy path code mapping to the records registry (e.g., `MCI-SEC-CR-DEPT-REG-SER-CERT`).
*   **`securityLevel` (enum):** Security classification governing access:
    *   `PUBLIC`: Viewable by anyone without authentication.
    *   `INTERNAL`: Restricted to authenticated MCI employees.
    *   `RESTRICTED`: Accessible only to the document owner and assigned caseworkers.
    *   `CONFIDENTIAL`: Restricted to department directors and executive officers.
*   **`ownerId` (string):** Unique ID of the citizen, merchant, or service account that uploaded or created the document.
*   **`currentVersion` (string):** Semantic version identifier (e.g., `v1.0.0`).
*   **`lifecycleStatus` (enum):** Current stage in the document lifecycle:
    *   `DRAFT` / `UNDER_REVIEW` / `APPROVED` / `ARCHIVED` / `RETIRED`.
*   **`qrVerificationId` (string):** Cryptographic verification ID linked to the secure validation landing page.
*   **`sha256Hash` (string):** SHA-256 checksum representing the document state to prevent unauthorized modifications.

## 4. DOCUMENT LIFECYCLE & VERSION CONTROL

All documents managed by the platform progress through a structured, auditable lifecycle.

```
 [ Draft Created ] ──► [ Under Review ] ──► [ Approved & Signed ] ──► [ Active Archive ] ──► [ Permanent Preservation / Disposal ]
```

### 4.1 Lifecycle Stages:
1.  **Draft / Intake:** Document is uploaded by an applicant or generated by a caseworker. At this stage, files are validated for integrity and run through an antivirus scanner.
2.  **Review / Audit:** The document is assigned to a caseworker for verification. Caseworkers can add minor comments or flag specific pages for correction.
3.  **Approval & Signing:** Once verified, the document is approved. If it is an official certificate (e.g., a Commercial Registry Extract), the system applies a cryptographic digital signature and generates a QR verification stamp.
4.  **Active Preservation:** The approved document enters the active registry. It can be viewed and shared by authorized users but cannot be modified.
5.  **Long-Term Archiving:** Retired or expired records are migrated to a read-only, cost-effective long-term storage vault.
6.  **Disposal / Permanent Retention:** Once the retention period expires, the document is either permanently deleted (e.g., for short-lived administrative drafts) or moved to the permanent national archives (e.g., for major historical company registers and ministerial decrees).

### 4.2 Version Control Standards
*   **Minor Versions (v0.x):** Used for initial drafts, edits, and caseworker comments during the preparation phase.
*   **Major Versions (v1.0.0, v2.0.0):** Created when a document is officially approved, signed, and published.
*   **Immutable Historical Records:** Once a major version is approved, it is locked and cannot be overwritten. Any subsequent updates (e.g., an amendment to a company's articles of incorporation) are saved as a new major version, preserving the complete administrative history.

## 5. DIGITAL SIGNATURES & QR VERIFICATION STAMPS

To ensure authentication and non-repudiation, the platform integrates digital signatures and QR-based verification.

```
                     ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
                     │ Certificate Generation (JSON Payload)  │
                     └───────────────────┬────────────────────┘
                                         │
                                         ▼
                     ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
                     │ Private Key Cryptographic Signing      │
                     └───────────────────┬────────────────────┘
                                         │
                                         ▼
                     ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
                     │ PDF Render with Secure QR Stamp Code   │
                     └────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 5.1 Cryptographic Signing Pipeline
1.  **Data Compilation:** The system compiles critical certificate details (such as Company Name, Registration Number, and Expiration Date) into a canonical JSON payload.
2.  **Signature Application:** The system hashes the payload and applies a digital signature using the Ministry's private key.
3.  **Bilingual Rendering:** The signed payload is rendered into an elegant, high-contrast PDF certificate using standard fonts (**DIN Next Arabic** for Arabic text and **DIN Next** for English text).
4.  **QR Stamp Placement:** A high-contrast QR stamp is embedded in the bottom-right corner of the certificate, linking to a secure validation URL (e.g., `https://mci.gov.sd/verify?hash=a8f3c2...`). This allows banks, customs, and third parties to instantly verify the document's authenticity.

## 6. RECORD RETENTION SCHEDULES

The platform utilizes configurable retention schedules to comply with Sudanese corporate laws and archival regulations. These schedules are managed dynamically to accommodate future policy changes.

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              Configurable Retention Rules              │
├───────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Commercial        │ 15 Years Active, followed by       │
│ Registries        │ Permanent National Preservation.   │
├───────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Industrial        │ 10 Years Active, followed by       │
│ Operating Licenses│ 5 Years Archive, then Disposal.    │
├───────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Consumer Fraud    │ 5 Years Active, followed by        │
│ Complaints        │ 5 Years Archive, then Disposal.    │
├───────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Audit Logs        │ 7 Years Active, followed by        │
│ & Security Feeds  │ Permanent Preservation.            │
└───────────────────┴────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Retention Schedule Definitions:
*   **RET-CR-01 (Commercial Registries):** Enforced for all company incorporations and articles of association. These files are kept in the active database for **15 years** after company dissolution, then migrated to the National Archives for permanent preservation.
*   **RET-IL-01 (Industrial Operating Licenses):** Kept in the active database for **10 years** from license expiration or cancellation, archived for **5 years**, and then permanently deleted if no active disputes or audits are outstanding.
*   **RET-CP-01 (Consumer Protection Records):** Kept in the active database for **5 years** from case resolution, archived for **5 years**, and then deleted.
*   **RET-SEC-01 (Audit Logs):** System audit trails and security logs must be kept in immutable, read-only storage for **7 years** to support security and compliance audits.

## 7. AI-ASSISTED DOCUMENT PROCESSING & SERVICES

Server-side Gemini models are integrated into the document management workflow to automate routine tasks, identify anomalies, and improve administrative efficiency.

```
[ Applicant Upload ] ──► [ AI OCR & Analysis ] ──► [ Policy & Validation Audit ] ──► [ Caseworker Review ]
```

### AI Document Services:
1.  **AI OCR & Data Extraction:** When an applicant uploads a document (e.g., a National ID or a bank receipt), the AI performs OCR to extract key text fields (such as Name, ID number, and Transaction Amount) and automatically populates the corresponding form fields.
2.  **Completeness & Compliance Check:** The AI scans uploaded articles of association against standard templates to verify that mandatory legal clauses and signatures are present.
3.  **Sensitive Data (PII) Redaction Suggestion:** The AI automatically flags sensitive information (such as personal phone numbers, home addresses, or bank account details) in documents scheduled for public release, suggesting appropriate redactions.
4.  **Semantic Search Integration:** The AI indexes the text and metadata of approved regulatory circulars, laws, and decrees, enabling users to perform natural language searches (e.g., *"كيف أسجل مصنع نسيج"* / *"What are the safety requirements for textile manufacturing?"*).
5.  **Advisory Limitation:** AI suggestions are **advisory only**. The AI is strictly forbidden from directly updating database records, approving applications, or rejecting files. All AI recommendations must be confirmed by an authorized caseworker.

## 8. SECURITY, PRIVACY & ROW-LEVEL ACCESS CONTROLS

To protect sensitive business and personal data, document access is secured through role-based and row-level controls.

```
                        [ Document Request ]
                                 │
                                 ▼
         ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
         │ Check IAM Role & Permissions                 │
         └──────────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                                │
                                ▼
         ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
         │ Apply Row-Level Filter (e.g., State = RedSea)│
         └──────────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                                │
                                ▼
                     [ Access Approved / Denied ]
```

### Security Controls:
1.  **Row-Level Security (RLS):** Document queries are restricted based on the user's role and regional jurisdiction. For example, a Regional Inspector for the Red Sea state can only view inspection files and evidence photos originating from their state's offices.
2.  **Watermarking:** Restricted or confidential documents viewed in the administrative workspace are dynamically watermarked with the viewing officer's name, IP address, and timestamp to prevent unauthorized sharing.
3.  **Download and Print Restrictions:** Confidential documents (such as pending investment proposals or investigative audits) can only be viewed within the secure browser-based document viewer. Downloading and printing of these files are blocked for standard caseworkers.
4.  **Immutable Access Logging:** Every access request (view, edit, download, print) is recorded in an immutable audit trail, capturing the user's ID, role, target document, timestamp, and IP address.

## 9. LEGAL EVIDENCE STANDARDS & NON-REPUDIATION

To ensure that digitized and system-generated documents are legally admissible in Sudanese courts, the platform complies with standard forensic evidence criteria.

*   **Cryptographic Chain of Custody:** The platform maintains a complete record of every action taken on a document (who uploaded it, who reviewed it, who approved it), with each transition cryptographically signed and timestamped.
*   **SHA-256 Verification:** The database stores a SHA-256 hash of every finalized document. If a file is tampered with on the storage server, its computed hash will not match the database record, instantly raising an alert and invalidating the document.
*   **Time-Stamping Authority (TSA):** Digital signatures are timestamped using a secure time source synced with the National Time Server, ensuring the validity of transaction records.
*   **Historical Preservation:** Expired or amended versions of documents are archived in a read-only state, ensuring that historical administrative records remain accessible for judicial audits.

## 10. RECORDS GOVERNANCE & COMPLIANCE

The EDRMF is managed under a structured governance framework to ensure administrative accuracy and compliance with changing regulations:

1.  **Document Ownership:** Every document classification series has an assigned owner (e.g., the Registrar General of the Corporate Registries Office is the owner of the `SER-CERT` series).
2.  **Records Officer Audit:** Designated Records Officers conduct semi-annual audits of document queues, checking classification accuracy, retention schedules, and archiving efficiency.
3.  **Disposal Approval Workflow:** No document can be deleted automatically upon retention expiration. The system flags expired files and sends them to the **Classification Committee** for final review and sign-off before permanent disposal.
4.  **Bilingual Governance Standards:** All system-generated documents and metadata schemas must maintain complete parity between Arabic and English, ensuring equal administrative access across languages.
